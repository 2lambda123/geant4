Sampling calorimeters

Description

The calorimeter is a box made of a given number of layers.
A layer consists of a sequence of various absorbers.
The pictures (G4) (G3) show a calorimeter of 15 layers. Each layer is 23 mm Iron and 0.4 mm Silicium.
The incident particle is an electron of 12 GeV. Only the tracks of charged particles are drawn.

Some results

  • (Pb-Fe-Si) calorimeter : visible energy. Geant4/Geant3/Experiment comparison
    The plot is the visible energy in silicium as a function of the energy of the incident electron.
    The experimental results are from Sicapo Col. NIM A332 (85-90) 1993

  • (Pb-Fe-Si) calorimeter : resolution. Geant4/Geant3/Experiment comparison
    In a slightly different setup, one plots the resolution on the visible energy in silicium as a function of the energy of the incident electron.
    The experimental results are from Sicapo Col. NIM A345 (244-249) 1994

The code of this example is in geant4/source/processes/electromagnetic/test/TestEm3
