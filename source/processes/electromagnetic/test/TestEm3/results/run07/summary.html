Production threshold expressed in range

In Geant4 the threshold for producing secondaries are expressed in range, universal for all medium. This range is converted in energy for each kind of particle and each material.
We illustrate here the advantages of this procedure.

Description

The setup is a sandwich of non-dense and dense materials:

Liquid Argon(4 mm)--Lead(4 mm)--Liquid Argon(4 mm)--Lead(4 mm)

The incident particle is a ionising particle: proton of 500 MeV.
The threshold to produce delta-rays is 1.5 mm. For an electron, it means:

Threshold in rangee- Energy in Liquid Argone- Energy in Lead
1.5 mm 455 keV 2 MeV

On the picture, one can see the delta-rays produced and travelling in Liquid Argon, while nothing in produced in Lead.
(on purpose the multiple scattering is off, and 1000 events are surimposed)

Geant3

On Geant3 one must set the cut for delta-rays (DCUTE) either to the Liquid Argon value, and producing many small unnecessary d-rays in Lead, or to the Lead value, and killing the d-rays production everywhere.

It should be noticed that the relevant physics quantities are quite stable versus the production threshold, both in Geant4 and in Geant3.
